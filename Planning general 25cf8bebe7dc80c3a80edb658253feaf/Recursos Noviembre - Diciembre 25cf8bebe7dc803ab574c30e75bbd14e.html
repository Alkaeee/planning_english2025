📘 Month 3 – Grammar & Topics

Week 9: Passive Voice (present, past, future)

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Present simple passive: The book is read by many people.
  • Past simple passive: The car was stolen yesterday.
  • Future passive: The project will be completed next year.
  • When to use: focus on the action/result, not the subject/agent.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Passive voice
  • British Council: Passive voice
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Passive exercises

Week 10: Relative Clauses (defining & non-defining)

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Defining: essential info.

    → The man who lives next door is very friendly.

  • Non-defining: extra info (use commas).

    → My brother, who lives in Spain, is an engineer.

  • Pronouns: who, which, that, whose, where.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Relative clauses
  • British Council: Relative clauses
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Relative clauses

Week 11: Gerunds & Infinitives

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • After certain verbs: enjoy doing, want to do, decide to do.
  • After prepositions: interested in learning.
  • As subject/object: Swimming is good for you.
  • Difference in meaning: stop to do vs stop doing.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Gerunds & infinitives
  • British Council: Verb patterns: verb + -ing or verb + infinitive
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Gerunds & infinitives

Week 12: Phrasal Verbs (common B2)

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Phrasal verbs = verb + particle (new meaning).
  • Examples:
    • give up = stop doing
    • look after = take care of
    • put off = postpone
    • come up with = think of an idea
  • Formal vs informal register.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Phrasal verbs
  • British Council: Phrasal verbs
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Phrasal verbs list & exercises
