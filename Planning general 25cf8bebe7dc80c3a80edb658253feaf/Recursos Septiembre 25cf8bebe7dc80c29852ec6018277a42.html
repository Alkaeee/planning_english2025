📘 Month 1 – Grammar & Topics

Week 1: Present Simple vs Present Continuous & Past Simple vs Past Continuous

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Present Simple: routines, facts, habits.

    → I usually get up at 7 a.m.

  • Present Continuous: actions happening now, temporary situations.

    → I’m studying English this year.

  • Past Simple: finished actions in the past.

    → I visited London last summer.

  • Past Continuous: ongoing actions in the past, interrupted actions.

    → I was watching TV when the phone rang.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Present Simple vs Present Continuous
  • British Council: Past Simple vs Past Continuous
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Mixed practice

Week 2: Present Perfect vs Past Simple

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Past Simple → finished time reference (yesterday, last year).

    → I went to Rome in 2022.

  • Present Perfect → life experience (no time reference), recent actions, or actions continuing until now.

    → I’ve been to Rome twice.

    → I’ve just had lunch.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Present Perfect vs Past Simple
  • British Council: Present Perfect Simple
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Present Perfect vs Past Simple

Week 3: Future Forms (will, going to, present continuous)

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Will → predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises.

    → I think it will rain tomorrow.

  • Going to → intentions, plans already decided, evidence.

    → I’m going to buy a new laptop.

  • Present Continuous (future meaning) → arranged events.

    → I’m meeting my friend at 6 p.m.

🔗 Resources

  • Cambridge: Will vs Going to
  • British Council: Talking about the future
  • Exercises: Perfect English Grammar – Future forms

Week 4: Comparatives & Superlatives + too / enough

🔹 Grammar Focus

  • Comparatives → smaller, more expensive

    → This phone is cheaper than that one.

  • Superlatives → the best, the most interesting

    → It’s the most beautiful city I’ve visited.

  • Too → negative connotation (too big = excessively big).
  • Enough → sufficient (not big enough = insufficient).
